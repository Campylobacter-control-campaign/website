About

Building on GETCampy

CCC builds on the GETCampy programme and related work across Africa and other international partner sites, bringing together clinical, veterinary, environmental, genomic, epidemiological, modelling and public-health expertise.

The project connects pathogen genomics with practical intervention strategies, including source attribution, decision-support modelling, improved surveillance and vaccine-informed prevention.

Aims

What CCC is trying to do

Map transmission

Characterise Campylobacter transmission across human, animal and environmental reservoirs.

Identify disease-associated lineages

Use genomic and metagenomic approaches to identify pathogen lineages and reservoirs associated with childhood disease.

Support source attribution

Develop and apply genomic source-attribution approaches in locally relevant settings.

Evaluate interventions

Combine genomic evidence with local knowledge to evaluate feasible strategies for reducing exposure and disease.

Explore upstream prevention

Investigate prevention strategies, including animal vaccination where appropriate, to reduce transmission at source.

Build equitable capacity

Support locally led surveillance, analysis, authorship, training and decision-making.
