# Campylobacter Control Campaign

The Campylobacter Control Campaign (CCC) is a One Health research and implementation network focused on understanding and reducing the burden of *Campylobacter* and other zoonotic enteric pathogens in high-transmission settings.

**Website:** https://campylobacter-control-campaign.github.io/website/

CCC brings together clinical, veterinary, environmental, genomic, epidemiological, modelling and public-health expertise. The programme builds on GETCampy and related work across Africa to connect pathogen genomics with practical interventions, including source attribution, decision-support modelling and vaccine-informed prevention strategies.

## Aims

- Characterise *Campylobacter* transmission across human, animal and environmental reservoirs.
- Identify pathogen lineages and reservoirs associated with childhood disease.
- Use genomic and metagenomic approaches to support surveillance and source attribution.
- Evaluate feasible interventions for reducing exposure and disease.
- Explore upstream prevention strategies, including animal vaccination, where appropriate.
- Build equitable, locally led surveillance and analytical capacity.
